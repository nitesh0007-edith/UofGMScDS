📋 Course Overview

Welcome to Information Retrieval! This course covers the fundamental concepts and advanced techniques behind modern search engines. You'll learn about:

🔍 Core Concepts

Text processing, indexing, and retrieval algorithms

📊 Evaluation

Measuring effectiveness with precision, recall, and more

🤖 Advanced Topics

Neural IR, learning to rank, and web search

⚡ Efficiency

Compression, pruning, and scalable architectures

📅 Course Timeline

Duration: 10 weeks (Jan 12 - Mar 16, 2026)

Assessment: 25% Coursework + 5% Quizzes + 70% Final Exam

Labs: PyTerrier hands-on exercises starting Week 3

📖 Recommended Textbooks

Book 1: Search Engines: Information Retrieval in Practice

Authors: Bruce Croft, Donald Metzler, Trevor Strohman

Publisher: Addison Wesley

Focus: Practical, implementation-focused approach to IR

Online Access: https://ciir.cs.umass.edu/irbook/

Best for: Understanding practical implementations, system architecture, and real-world applications

Book 2: Introduction to Information Retrieval

Authors: Christopher Manning, Prabhakar Raghavan, Hinrich Schütze

Publisher: Cambridge University Press

Focus: Comprehensive theoretical foundation with mathematical rigor

Online Access: https://nlp.stanford.edu/IR-book/

Best for: Deep theoretical understanding, mathematical foundations, and comprehensive coverage

📚 How to Use These Books

Complementary Approach

  • Book 1 (Croft et al.): Read first for practical understanding and implementation details
  • Book 2 (Manning et al.): Use for deeper theoretical grounding and mathematical proofs
  • Strategy: Start with Book 1 for the week's topic, then dive into Book 2 for deeper understanding
  • Exercises: Both books have excellent exercises - try problems from both

🎯 Recommended Study Approach

Weekly Study Cycle

Monday - Pre-Lecture (2-3 hours)

  • Read assigned chapters from Book 1 (practical perspective)
  • Skim corresponding sections in Book 2
  • Note down questions and unclear concepts

During Lecture (3 hours)

  • Active note-taking using Cornell method
  • Ask questions about pre-reading
  • Link concepts to textbook examples

Tuesday-Thursday - Deep Study (4-5 hours)

  • Re-read Book 2 sections for theoretical depth
  • Work through textbook examples
  • Solve end-of-chapter exercises
  • Create concept maps and summaries

Friday - Lab Session + Practice (3-4 hours)

  • Attend lab and complete PyTerrier exercises
  • Implement concepts from lectures
  • Experiment with variations

Weekend - Review & Consolidate (2-3 hours)

  • Review week's materials
  • Complete any unfinished exercises
  • Prepare for next week's topics
  • Work on coursework assignments

🎓 Learning Strategies That Work

  • Active Recall: Test yourself regularly without looking at notes
  • Spaced Repetition: Review concepts at increasing intervals
  • Feynman Technique: Explain concepts in simple terms as if teaching someone
  • Practice with Real Data: Use PyTerrier with different datasets
  • Connect Concepts: Build a concept map showing relationships between topics

Week 1: Introduction and IR Systems Architecture

Jan 12-16, 2026

Fundamental

  • Chapter 3: Crawls and Feeds

📖 Book 2 (Manning et al.)

  • Chapter 1: Boolean retrieval
  • Chapter 2: The term vocabulary and postings lists
  • Chapter 3: Dictionaries and tolerant retrieval
  • Chapter 4: Index construction

💡 Key Focus Areas

  • Understand the difference between IR and database queries
  • Master inverted index structure and construction
  • Learn text processing: tokenization, stemming, stopwords
  • Grasp the concept of document representation (bag of words)

✏️ Practice Exercises

  • Build a small inverted index by hand (Book 2, Ch 1 exercises)
  • Compare different stemming algorithms
  • Analyze the space complexity of different index structures

Week 2: Term Weighting Models (Vector Space)

Jan 19-23, 2026

Fundamental

📖 Book 1 (Croft et al.)

  • Chapter 7: Retrieval Models (Focus on Vector Space Model)
  • Section 7.1: An Overview of Retrieval Models

📖 Book 2 (Manning et al.)

  • Chapter 6: Scoring, term weighting, and the vector space model
  • Section 6.2: The vector space model for scoring
  • Section 6.3: Variant tf-idf functions
  • Section 6.4: Document and query weighting schemes

💡 Key Focus Areas

  • Master TF-IDF weighting scheme and its variants
  • Understand cosine similarity for document ranking
  • Learn document length normalization techniques
  • Grasp the geometric interpretation of vector space

✏️ Practice Exercises

  • Calculate TF-IDF scores for sample documents by hand
  • Implement different normalization schemes
  • Compare rankings with different term weighting schemes

Time Management

⏰ Recommended Weekly Time Investment

  • Lectures: 3 hours (mandatory)
  • Pre-reading: 2-3 hours
  • Deep study & exercises: 4-5 hours
  • Lab work: 3-4 hours
  • Review & consolidation: 2-3 hours
  • Total: ~15-18 hours per week

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

⚠️ Don't Fall Into These Traps

  • Passive reading: Always take notes and work examples
  • Cramming: Consistent daily study beats last-minute cramming
  • Skipping labs: Hands-on practice is crucial for understanding
  • Ignoring math: Work through mathematical derivations, don't skip them
  • Not asking questions: Use office hours and forums actively
  • Delaying coursework: Start assignments early, don't wait until deadline
