Generative Adversarial Networks

Two networks in competition—a generator creates fake data while a discriminator tries to detect it. Together they produce stunningly realistic outputs.

← Previous Next →

The GAN Concept

Two networks play a minimax game:

📖 GAN Components

Generator G: Takes random noise z, produces fake data G(z)
Discriminator D: Classifies data as real or fake

G tries to fool D. D tries not to be fooled.

min_G max_D E[log D(x)] + E[log(1 - D(G(z)))]

Training GANs

Alternate between training D and G:

  1. Train D: Show real and fake data, update D to classify correctly
  2. Train G: Generate fake data, update G to fool D
  3. Repeat until equilibrium
⚠️ Training Challenges

Mode collapse: G produces limited variety
Training instability: D wins too easily or G wins too easily
Evaluation: Hard to measure generation quality

GAN Variants

  • DCGAN: Convolutional, stable training techniques
  • Conditional GAN: Control generation with labels
  • StyleGAN: High-quality face generation with style control
  • CycleGAN: Unpaired image-to-image translation
  • Pix2Pix: Paired image-to-image translation

Applications

  • Photorealistic image synthesis
  • Image super-resolution
  • Style transfer and domain adaptation
  • Data augmentation
  • Video generation

Key Takeaways

  • GANs: Generator vs Discriminator in adversarial game
  • Generator learns to create realistic data from noise
  • Training is challenging: mode collapse, instability
  • StyleGAN achieves remarkable face synthesis quality
  • Applications: synthesis, translation, super-resolution
