Deep Learning and Ethics

The critical responsibility that comes with powerful technology—bias, fairness, privacy, and the societal impact of AI.

← PreviousNext →

Bias and Fairness

Models can perpetuate and amplify societal biases:

  • Data bias: Training data reflects historical inequities
  • Representation bias: Some groups underrepresented
  • Measurement bias: Labels may be biased
⚠️ Real Consequences

Biased AI affects hiring, lending, healthcare, criminal justice. These systems impact real lives and can perpetuate discrimination.

Interpretability

Understanding why models make decisions is crucial for:

  • Debugging and improving models
  • Building trust with users
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Scientific discovery

Privacy

Concerns include:

  • Models memorizing training data
  • Membership inference attacks
  • Model inversion attacks

Solutions: differential privacy, federated learning.

Potential for Misuse

  • Deepfakes and misinformation
  • Surveillance and tracking
  • Autonomous weapons
  • Manipulation and persuasion

Key Takeaways

  • AI systems can encode and amplify biases
  • Fairness has multiple definitions—context matters
  • Interpretability aids trust and debugging
  • Privacy requires active protection
  • Consider downstream impacts of your work
  • Ethics is not optional—it's essential
